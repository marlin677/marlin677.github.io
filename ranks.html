INTERNAL DOCUMENT 04-A

Rank Structure & Duties

National Fire Commander

Oversees all operations across the country. Responsible for national strategy, government relations, and ultimate department accountability.

Assistant National Fire Commander

Supports the National Commander in daily operations and assumes command of major large-scale national emergencies.

District Manager Ben Lewis

Responsible for the strategic management of the Christchurch District, ensuring resource allocation and personnel welfare.

Group Manager Ethan Brown

Coordinates multiple stations within a designated group, bridging the gap between local stations and district leadership.

Senior Station Officer (SSO)

The ranking officer on shift. Manages station administration and acts as the Incident Controller (IC) at significant callouts.

Station Officer (SO)

Commands a single appliance and crew. Responsible for the safety, training, and tactical deployment of their firefighters.

Senior Firefighter

Highly experienced frontline personnel who provide mentorship to junior staff and lead specific tactical tasks on-scene.

Qualified Firefighter

Full-time personnel who have completed all core competencies and are authorized to operate advanced equipment.

Firefighter

Personnel currently in the development phase, working under supervision to master firefighting and rescue techniques.

Volunteer Firefighter

Community-based responders who provide vital emergency services. They maintain the same standards of training as career staff.

Ops Support Volunteer

Provides non-firefighting support including traffic control, lighting, and logistics to ensure scene safety and efficiency.

EXCLUSIVE RANKS
Chief Fire Officer (CFO)

The highest-ranking officer within a specific volunteer brigade. Holds ultimate responsibility for the brigade's performance and personnel.

Deputy Chief Fire Officer (DCFO)

Second-in-command of a volunteer brigade. Manages training schedules and assists the CFO in operational leadership.
